Antenna Basics

Back to Guides

Simple antenna basics that help you get better results without overcomplicating it.

The antenna matters most

A better antenna system usually improves results more than upgrading radios. Start simple, get on air, then improve one thing at a time.

Three basics that always help

  • Height helps, especially for VHF and UHF.
  • Clear space helps, away from metal roofs, gutters, and power lines.
  • Good feedline helps, especially on VHF and above, where coax loss can be brutal.

Coax and connectors

  • Use the best coax you can for the run length and band, short runs matter less than long runs.
  • Weatherproof outdoor joins, water in coax causes problems fast.
  • Use strain relief, connectors should not carry weight.

SWR, what it is, and what it is not

  • Low SWR can protect your radio and keep power transfer efficient.
  • Perfect SWR does not guarantee performance, pattern and height matter too.
  • If SWR is high, stop and check connectors, coax, and antenna length.
Practical tip
Fix safety and reliability first, then tune for SWR, then chase performance.

Simple starter ideas

VHF, UHF
A vertical antenna as high as safely possible, good coax, and a clear view.
HF
A simple wire antenna in the clear, a decent feedpoint, and patience while you learn the band.

Safety, this comes first

  • Keep antennas well clear of power lines, always.
  • Secure masts and guy lines, wind loads can be serious.
  • Grounding and lightning protection should be planned for your situation.
