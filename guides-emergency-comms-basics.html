Emergency Comms Basics

Back to Guides

Clear, calm habits for message passing, priority traffic, and directed nets.

The goal, clear communication

In emergency style traffic, clarity and calm matter more than radio jargon. Keep messages short, accurate, and confirmed.

Directed nets, how they usually work

  • Net control station (NCS) manages the flow, wait to be called in.
  • Use short transmissions, identify clearly, then stand by.
  • If you double with someone, stop, and try again when invited.
Example check in
Net control, ZL3TOM, Christchurch, no traffic.

Message handling basics

  • Who it is to, who it is from, and the message content.
  • Repeat critical details, names, addresses, phone numbers, and counts.
  • Read back key parts, confirm what you copied.
A simple format
To: [name or agency]
From: [name or station]
Message: [short, clear content]
Time: [UTC], Date: [UTC]

Priority and emergency breaks

  • Only use priority or emergency breaks when it is genuinely needed.
  • Keep your wording short and clear so net control can respond fast.

Voice discipline that helps everyone

  • Use phonetics for names and locations when copy is weak.
  • Pause between overs, especially on linked systems with delay.
  • If you are relaying, say you are relaying, and for who.

What not to do

  • Do not guess callsigns, names, or numbers, ask for repeats.
  • Do not chat on a directed net unless invited by net control.
  • Do not transmit long overs, keep air time available.
