Amateur Radio Operating Basics

Back to Guides

A practical guide to operating confidently across HF, VHF, UHF, repeaters, digital modes, and linked systems. CQ is covered where it applies.

Before you transmit

  • Listen first and confirm the frequency or repeater is clear.
  • Set audio and power levels cleanly for the mode you are using.
  • Know whether the frequency is a calling channel, repeater, net, or digital segment.
  • Have your log ready with time (UTC), band, mode, and callsign.

HF operation and calling CQ

CQ is mainly used on HF and some digital modes. Keep calls clear and unhurried. 

CQ CQ CQ, this is ZL3TOM, ZL3TOM calling CQ and standing by.

VHF and UHF simplex

On VHF and UHF, calls are often made on a calling channel, then moved off. 

ZL3TOM listening.

Repeaters and linked systems

  • Pause briefly after keying up so links activate.
  • Identify clearly and invite replies.
  • Keep transmissions concise, especially on wide area systems.

Digital modes

  • Clean audio matters more than power.
  • Follow band plans and recommended frequencies.
  • Monitor your signal and stop if you cause interference.

Logging and good practice

  • Log contacts accurately using UTC.
  • Identify regularly and at the end of your over.
  • Leave pauses so others can join or break in.
  • Be welcoming, patient, and respectful.
