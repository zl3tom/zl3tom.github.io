Guides

Audio and Levels

Back to Guides

Quick, practical settings and habits for clean audio on SSB, FM, and digital modes.

The goal, sound clean

On every mode, the easiest station to work is the station that sounds clean and steady. Loud does not equal good, clean equals good.

SSB mic gain and processing

  • Set mic gain so your voice is strong but not distorted.
  • If you have compression or speech processing, use it lightly, too much makes you harsh.
  • Keep a consistent mic distance, and speak across the mic to reduce pops.
Quick check
If people say “you are splattering” or “your audio is crunchy”, reduce gain or processing.

FM deviation and handheld habits

  • Normal voice, normal distance, most handhelds work best that way.
  • Talking too close often causes muffled audio, or clipping.
  • On repeaters, key up, pause, then talk, it helps links and courtesy tones.

Digital modes, drive level is everything

  • Start with low audio drive, increase slowly.
  • Aim for minimal or no ALC movement on many radios, heavy ALC often means distortion.
  • Turn off speech processing for soundcard digital modes.
The simple rule
Clean digital beats extra power, every time.

CW sidetone and filtering

  • Set sidetone pitch to a comfortable frequency so you can copy longer.
  • Use receiver filters to reduce noise, but do not make it so narrow you miss replies.

Mini checklist you can use any time

SSB: no distortion, light processing
FM: normal deviation, do not shout
Digital: low drive, minimal ALC
All: steady pace, clear callsign
