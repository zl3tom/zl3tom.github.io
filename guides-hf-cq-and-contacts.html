Guides

HF CQ and Contacts

Back to Guides

Practical tips for HF calling, answering, handling busy conditions, and finishing a QSO cleanly.

1) Find a clear frequency

  • Listen first, at least 10 to 20 seconds, longer if it sounds busy or weak.
  • Check the context, some frequencies are used for nets, DX activity, or common calling spots.
  • If you are unsure, ask once, then listen again.
Example
Is this frequency in use, is this frequency in use, ZL3TOM.

2) Calling CQ (SSB)

Short beats fancy. Repeat your callsign clearly, then listen.

Example CQ
CQ CQ CQ, this is ZL3TOM, ZL3TOM calling CQ and standing by.
  • Call once or twice, then listen. Avoid constant calling.
  • If nobody answers, change slightly, add your location once, then listen again.

3) Answering a CQ

  • Say their callsign once, then your callsign once.
  • Slow is smooth, smooth is fast.
Example
ZL3ABC, this is ZL3TOM.

4) The first over, what to say

Keep it easy to log, callsign confirmation, report, name, location.

Example
ZL3ABC, thanks for the call, you are 5 and 9 in Christchurch, name Thomas, over.

5) Signal reports, real world tips

  • On SSB you will often hear “59” style reports, even when signals are not perfect, do what feels fair.
  • If they are weak, say it kindly, for example: “You are 4 and 7, readable with a bit of noise.”
  • If copy is hard, ask for repeats, do not guess callsigns.

6) Handling pileups

  • Take one station at a time, confirm the callsign before you log it.
  • Use short overs, it keeps the frequency flowing.
  • If you cannot copy, say so, “Again your callsign please,” and listen.
Useful line
Only the station ending in ABC, again please.

7) Ending cleanly, and staying on frequency

  • Thank them, confirm final callsigns, then sign off.
  • If you are staying put, say so, then call CQ or “QRZ” after a pause.
Example sign off
Thanks for the contact, 73, this is ZL3TOM clear and listening.
